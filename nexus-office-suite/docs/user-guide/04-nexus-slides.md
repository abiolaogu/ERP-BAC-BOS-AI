## Working with Slides

### Adding Slides

#### New Blank Slide
- Click **"+ Slide"** button (top toolbar)
- Or press `Ctrl+M`
- Or right-click thumbnail â†’ **"New slide"**

#### New Slide with Layout
1. Click dropdown arrow next to **"+ Slide"**
2. Choose layout:
   - **Title slide**: Large title, subtitle
   - **Title and body**: Title with content area
   - **Title only**: Just a title
   - **Section header**: Divider slide
   - **Two columns**: Split content
   - **Comparison**: Side-by-side comparison
   - **Blank**: Empty slide
3. Click to insert

#### Duplicate Slide
- Right-click slide thumbnail
- Select **"Duplicate slide"**
- Or select slide and press `Ctrl+D`

### Organizing Slides

#### Reorder Slides
- **In slide panel**: Drag thumbnail to new position
- **In grid view**: Click **View** â†’ **Grid view**, then drag

#### Delete Slides
- Click slide thumbnail
- Press `Delete` key
- Or right-click â†’ **"Delete slide"**

#### Skip Slides
Don't delete, just hide from presentation:
- Right-click slide thumbnail
- Select **"Skip slide"**
- Grayed out thumbnail indicates skipped
- Uncheck to include again

#### Group Slides into Sections
1. Right-click slide thumbnail
2. Select **"New section"**
3. Name the section
4. Drag slides into section
5. Collapse/expand sections

## Tips and Best Practices

### 1. Design Principles

**Simplicity**
- One idea per slide
- Minimal text (30-40 words max)
- Plenty of white space

**Contrast**
- High contrast for readability
- Dark text on light background or vice versa
- Avoid low-contrast color combinations

**Consistency**
- Use theme for consistent look
- Same fonts throughout
- Align objects properly

**Hierarchy**
- Largest text for most important info
- Use headings and subheadings
- Visual hierarchy guides attention

### 2. Content Guidelines

**Text**
- Use bullet points, not paragraphs
- 6 words per line, 6 lines per slide (6Ã—6 rule)
- Font size minimum 24pt for body text

**Images**
- High resolution (300 DPI for print)
- Relevant to content
- Use alt text for accessibility

**Colors**
- 3-5 colors maximum
- Use theme colors
- Consider color-blind accessibility

**Fonts**
- 2 fonts maximum (heading + body)
- Sans-serif for modern, clean look
- Serif for traditional, formal presentations

### 3. Presentation Tips

**Preparation**
- Rehearse with timer
- Test equipment beforehand
- Have backup (USB drive, cloud link)
- Print speaker notes

**Delivery**
- Make eye contact with audience
- Speak to audience, not slides
- Use slides as visual aid, not teleprompter
- Pause for impact

**Engagement**
- Ask questions
- Use audience Q&A
- Include interactive elements
- Tell stories, not just facts

### 4. Accessibility

- Use alt text for images
- Ensure color contrast (WCAG AA standard)
- Don't rely on color alone
- Provide captions for videos
- Use readable fonts (avoid script/decorative)

## Troubleshooting

### Slides not loading?
- Check internet connection
- Refresh page (F5)
- Clear browser cache
- Try different browser

### Font looks different?
- Custom fonts may not be installed
- Use theme fonts for consistency
- Embed fonts when downloading

### Video not playing?
- Check video URL is valid
- Ensure video is publicly accessible
- Try re-inserting video
- Download presentation and play locally

### Can't present?
- Click **"Present"** button
- Check browser allows full-screen
- Try presenter view instead
- Use different browser (Chrome recommended)

### Animations not working?
- Animations work in present mode only
- Not visible in edit mode
- Check animation settings
