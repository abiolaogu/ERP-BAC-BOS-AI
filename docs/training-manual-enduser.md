## 1. Training Overview

This training manual helps end users learn to use the NEXUS Business Operating System effectively for their daily business operations. Training is organized into progressive modules covering core workflows.

### 1.1 Training Objectives
Upon completion, end users will be able to:
- Navigate the NEXUS platform confidently
- Manage contacts, leads, and sales opportunities in CRM
- Create and track invoices and payments
- Collaborate using the Office Suite (Mail, Drive, Writer, Chat, Meet)
- Use the AI Copilot for productivity assistance
- Manage projects, tasks, and team collaboration

### 1.2 Training Schedule
| Module | Duration | Format |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Module 1: Getting Started | 1 hour | Self-paced video |
| Module 2: CRM Essentials | 2 hours | Instructor-led + exercises |

## 2. Module 1: Getting Started

### 2.1 Lesson: First Login
**Objective**: Log in and set up your account.

**Steps**:
1. Open the invitation email from your administrator
2. Click the activation link
3. Set your password (minimum 12 characters, mixed case, at least one number)
4. Set up MFA if required (scan QR code with authenticator app)
5. Log in to the platform

**Exercise**: Complete your first login and reach the dashboard.

### 2.2 Lesson: Dashboard Tour
**Objective**: Understand the dashboard layout and navigation.

**Key Areas**:
- **Top Bar**: Search (Ctrl/Cmd+K), notifications bell, AI Copilot button, profile avatar
- **Left Sidebar**: Module navigation (CRM, Finance, Projects, HR, Office, etc.)
- **Main Area**: Dashboard widgets showing your activity, calendar, and quick actions
- **Bottom Right**: AI Copilot floating button for instant help

**Exercise**: Locate and click on each module in the sidebar. Return to the dashboard using the Home icon.

### 2.3 Lesson: Profile Setup
**Objective**: Configure your personal profile and preferences.

**Steps**:
1. Click your avatar > Profile
2. Upload a profile photo
3. Enter your phone number and job title
4. Set your timezone and language preference
5. Configure notification preferences (which events trigger email vs. in-app vs. push)
6. Set up your email signature for the Mail module

**Exercise**: Complete your profile setup with all required fields.

## 3. Module 2: CRM Essentials

### 3.1 Lesson: Creating Your First Contact
**Objective**: Add and manage business contacts.

**Steps**:
1. Navigate to CRM > Contacts
2. Click + New Contact
3. Fill in: First name, Last name, Email, Phone, Company
4. Add tags (e.g., "VIP", "Lead Source: Website")
5. Add custom fields if needed
6. Click Save

**Exercise**: Create 5 contacts with different tags and companies.

### 3.2 Lesson: Working the Sales Pipeline
**Objective**: Manage leads from creation through conversion.

**Workflow**:
```
New Lead -> Contact Made -> Qualified -> Proposal Sent -> Negotiation -> Closed Won/Lost
```

**Steps**:
1. Navigate to CRM > Leads > + New Lead
2. Enter lead details and source (website, referral, event, etc.)
3. Update lead status as you progress through conversations
4. When qualified, click "Convert to Opportunity"
5. Set opportunity value, expected close date, and probability
6. Move through pipeline stages on the visual board

**Exercise**: Create 3 leads, qualify 2, and convert 1 to an opportunity. Move the opportunity through pipeline stages to "Closed Won."

### 3.3 Lesson: CRM Search and Filters
**Objective**: Find contacts and leads quickly.

**Techniques**:
- **Quick Search**: Type in the search bar at the top of any CRM page
- **Advanced Filters**: Click the filter icon to combine criteria (tags, dates, custom fields)
- **Saved Views**: Save frequently used filter combinations for one-click access
- **Export**: Select contacts and export to CSV for reporting

**Exercise**: Create a saved view for "VIP Contacts Added This Month."

## 4. Module 3: Finance Basics

### 4.1 Lesson: Creating an Invoice
**Objective**: Generate and send professional invoices.

**Steps**:
1. Navigate to Finance > Invoices > + New Invoice
2. Select or create the customer (linked from CRM contacts)
3. Add line items: description, quantity, unit price
4. Apply tax rate if applicable
5. Add payment terms and due date
6. Preview the invoice
7. Click Send to email the invoice with a payment link

**Exercise**: Create an invoice for a fictional client with 3 line items and send it.

### 4.2 Lesson: Tracking Payments
**Objective**: Monitor payment status and record manual payments.

**Dashboard Views**:
- **Outstanding**: Invoices awaiting payment
- **Overdue**: Invoices past their due date
- **Paid**: Successfully paid invoices
- **All**: Complete invoice history

**Steps for Manual Payment Recording**:
1. Open the invoice
2. Click "Record Payment"
3. Enter amount received, date, and payment method
4. The invoice status updates automatically

**Exercise**: Record a partial payment against the invoice created in 4.1.

### 4.3 Lesson: Financial Reports
**Objective**: Generate and interpret financial reports.

**Available Reports**:
- Revenue Summary: Total revenue by period
- Accounts Receivable: Outstanding amounts by customer
- Payment History: All transactions with dates and methods

**Exercise**: Generate a Revenue Summary report for the current month and export as PDF.

## 5. Module 4: Office Suite

### 5.1 Lesson: Email (Mail)
**Objective**: Send, receive, and organize emails.

**Steps**:
1. Navigate to Office > Mail
2. Click Compose to write a new email
3. Add recipients, subject, and body with formatting
4. Attach files from Drive or your computer
5. Send the email

**Organization Tips**:
- Create folders for different projects or clients
- Use labels for quick categorization
- Set up filters for automatic email sorting
- Use the search bar for finding old emails

**Exercise**: Send a test email with an attachment. Create a folder and move the email into it.

### 5.2 Lesson: File Management (Drive)
**Objective**: Store, organize, and share files.

**Steps**:
1. Navigate to Office > Drive
2. Create a folder structure (e.g., Projects > ClientA > Proposals)
3. Upload files via drag-and-drop or the upload button
4. Share a file: Right-click > Share > Enter colleague's email > Set permission (View/Edit)
5. Access version history: Right-click > Version History

**Exercise**: Create a project folder, upload 3 files, and share the folder with a colleague.

### 5.3 Lesson: Document Collaboration (Writer)
**Objective**: Create documents and collaborate in real-time.

**Steps**:
1. Navigate to Office > Writer > + New Document
2. Name the document and start writing
3. Use the toolbar for formatting (headings, bold, lists, tables)
4. Click Share and invite a colleague to co-edit
5. See their cursor and edits in real-time
6. Export to PDF when finished

**Exercise**: Create a meeting notes document and invite a training partner to co-edit it.

### 5.4 Lesson: Meetings (Calendar and Meet)
**Objective**: Schedule and conduct video meetings.

**Steps**:
1. Navigate to Office > Calendar
2. Click on a time slot to create a new event
3. Add title, description, and attendees
4. A Meet link is automatically generated
5. At the meeting time, click the Meet link to join
6. Use video, audio, screen sharing, and chat during the meeting

**Exercise**: Schedule a test meeting for tomorrow and invite a colleague.

### 5.5 Lesson: Team Chat
**Objective**: Communicate in real-time with your team.

**Steps**:
1. Navigate to Office > Chat
2. Start a direct message by clicking + and selecting a colleague
3. Create a channel for team or project discussions
4. Use threads for organized conversations on specific topics
5. Share files directly in chat messages

**Exercise**: Create a project channel and send a message with a file attachment.

## 6. Module 5: Projects and Tasks

### 6.1 Lesson: Creating a Project
**Objective**: Set up and manage a project from start to finish.

**Steps**:
1. Navigate to Projects > + New Project
2. Enter project name, description, and date range
3. Add team members
4. Choose default view: Kanban (recommended for beginners), List, Gantt, or Calendar

**Exercise**: Create a sample project called "Q1 Marketing Campaign."

### 6.2 Lesson: Task Management
**Objective**: Create, assign, and track tasks.

**Steps**:
1. Within your project, click + Add Task
2. Set: Title, description, assignee, priority (Low/Medium/High/Critical), due date
3. Add tags and checklists for sub-tasks
4. On the Kanban board, drag tasks between columns as work progresses
5. Add comments to tasks for team discussion

**Exercise**: Create 5 tasks in your project, assign them to team members, and move 2 to "In Progress."

### 6.3 Lesson: Project Tracking
**Objective**: Monitor project progress and team productivity.

**Views**:
- **Kanban**: Visual card-based workflow
- **Gantt**: Timeline view showing dependencies and milestones
- **List**: Sortable and filterable table view
- **Calendar**: Tasks plotted on a calendar by due date

**Exercise**: Switch between all four views and identify overdue tasks.

## 7. Module 6: AI Copilot

### 7.1 Lesson: Introduction to the AI Copilot
**Objective**: Understand what the AI Copilot can do and how to interact with it.

**Access**: Click the AI icon (bottom-right corner) or press Ctrl/Cmd+/

**Capabilities**:
- Answer questions about your business data
- Generate reports and summaries
- Draft emails, documents, and messages
- Provide analysis and recommendations
- Help navigate the platform

### 7.2 Lesson: Effective Prompting
**Objective**: Get the best results from the AI Copilot.

**Good Prompts** (Specific, actionable):
- "Show me the top 10 customers by revenue this quarter"
- "Draft a follow-up email to John Smith about the proposal we discussed on Monday"
- "Create a summary of all overdue tasks in the Q1 Marketing project"
- "What is our average payment collection time for invoices this month?"

**Less Effective Prompts** (Too vague):
- "Show me data"
- "Help with email"
- "What about the project?"

**Exercise**: Ask the AI Copilot 5 questions about different modules (CRM, Finance, Projects, HR, Inventory) and evaluate the responses.

### 7.3 Lesson: AI-Assisted Workflows
**Objective**: Use AI to accelerate common workflows.

**Scenarios**:
1. "Analyze my sales pipeline and recommend which deals to focus on this week"
2. "Generate a weekly status report for my project based on completed tasks"
3. "Find all contacts who haven't been contacted in 30 days and draft outreach emails"
4. "Create an inventory reorder report for products below minimum stock levels"

**Exercise**: Complete one AI-assisted workflow end-to-end.

## 8. Module 7: Advanced Features

### 8.1 Lesson: Keyboard Shortcuts
| Shortcut | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Ctrl/Cmd + K | Global search |
| Ctrl/Cmd + N | New item (context-dependent) |
| Ctrl/Cmd + / | Open AI Copilot |
| Ctrl/Cmd + Shift + M | Open Mail |
| Ctrl/Cmd + Shift + C | Open Chat |
| Ctrl/Cmd + S | Save current item |
| Ctrl/Cmd + Enter | Send (in Mail or Chat) |

### 8.2 Lesson: Data Import and Export
**Import**: CRM > Contacts > Import > Upload CSV > Map columns > Confirm
**Export**: Select items > Actions > Export > Choose format (CSV, PDF, Excel)

### 8.3 Lesson: Notifications and Alerts
Configure what triggers notifications in Profile > Notification Settings:
- Task assignments and due dates
- Invoice payments and overdue alerts
- CRM lead assignments
- Calendar event reminders
- Chat mentions and direct messages
